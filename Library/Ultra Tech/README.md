# Ultra-Tech notes

## Deviations

Some weapons in Ultra-Tech have impossible reload weights from the total weight
of the ammunition equalling or exceeding the reload weight, requiring magazines
that are weightless or less.

[Chandley's figures][] are used to correct obviously wrong reload weights since
they use the rules from the unreleased Ultra-Tech playtest combined with figures
from the playtest for tube feed and belts, and deriving the figure for light
magazines from the 7mm conventional guns.

Chandley's loaded weight modifications are required because some guns (e.g.
Gauss Minineedler) have fully loaded weights equal to the total weight of the
ammo and it is incongruous for the weapon to be weightless.

Increased gun weights are included in the unloaded weight modifier instead of
increasing the base item weight so that RAW weights are usable when not opting
into magazine tracking.

**This means the loaded weight of the weapon with and without the modifier
intentionally may not match.**

Guns with reload weights that result in positive-weight magazines are left with
Ultra-Tech stats since consistency with RAW is preferred over consistency
between weapons.

The list of deviations is:

* Anti-Materiel Rifle, 15mmCL uses Chandley's 0.22 lb reload weight and 30.2 lb loaded weight since the sum of the weight of the ammo otherwise equals the reload weight.
* Gauss Minineedler uses Chandley's 0.11 lb reload weight and 0.21 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the loaded ammo equals the entire loaded weight.
* Gyroc Carbine, 15mm uses 1.1 lb reload weight and 4.1 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo equals the entire loaded weight. This figure is derived from the rules Chandley used as Gyrocs aren't listed.
* Gyroc Launch Pistol, 15mm uses 0.77 lb reload weight and 2.07 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo equals the entire loaded weight. This figure is derived from the rules Chandley used as Gyrocs aren't listed.
* Gyroc LSW, 15mm uses 3.15 lb reload weight and 12.15 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo equals the entire loaded weight. Chandley didn't list the multiplier for belt-fed but 1.05 matches for Heavy Chaingun and LSW.
* Ice Gun, 3mm ice uses 0.6 lb reload weight and 1.3 lb loaded weight. Ice ammo weight is derived from the weight of a cup of water and gives 0.5 lbs total ammo weight, which is above Chandley's lower figure to the higher figure is used.
* Light Support Weapon, 7mmCL uses Chandley's 5.7 lb reload weight and 15.7 lb loaded weight since the sum of the weight of the ammo otherwise exceeds the reload weight.
* Minigun, 7mmCL uses Chandley's 11 lb belt-fed reload weight and 34 lb loaded weight since the sum of the weight of the ammo otherwise exceeds the reload weight.
* Needler, 3mmN uses 0.4 lb reload weight and 1.1 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo equals the entire loaded weight.
* Portable Railgun, 10mm uses 2.7 lb reload weight and 21.2 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo exceeds the entire loaded weight. This differs from Chandley's listed stats since they include cell weight that we track separately.
* Storm Carbine, 10mmCL uses 2.2 lb reload weight and 8.2 lb loaded weight since the sum of the weight of the ammo otherwise equals the reload weight.
* Underbarrel Grenade Launcher, 40mmPLB uses 2.2 lb reload weight and 3.2 lb loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo equals the entire loaded weight.
* Wrist Needler, 3mmN uses 0.14 reload weight and 0.24 loaded weight since otherwise the weight of the ammo exceeds the entire loaded weight.
